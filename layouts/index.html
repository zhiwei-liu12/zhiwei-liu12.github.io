Bio

I am an undergraduate student at Renmin University of China, majoring in Data Computing and Applications (ranked 1/22). My research focuses on efficient training and inference for large language models, particularly KV cache compression, attention mechanism design, and cross-layer communication budget allocation in Transformer architectures.

I am currently working on IA-KV (Interface-Aware KV Compression), a project that reframes KV compression as a cross-layer communication capacity allocation problem. I am also first author of a study on heterogeneous RoPE dimension allocation in MLA architectures, and a contributor to QK-Coupling (NeurIPS 2026).

Research Interests

KV Cache Compression

Cross-layer communication budget allocation, interface layer detection, non-uniform compression strategies for MLA/GQA/MQA architectures.

Attention Mechanism Design

Heterogeneous positional encoding (RoPE) allocation, QK coupling and shared attention routing, efficient attention architectures.

Efficient LLM Training

Compression-induced interface formation, MoE routing and attention coupling, parameter-efficient training paradigms.
